# US History Standards Overview

**Course:** English II + US History (Grade 10)
**Standards:** US.6_12.1 through US.6_12.6

---

## The Key Fact: Six Standards, Nine Units

US History in SDA uses the same logic as World History: **six standards that repeat across all nine units**. The standard code stays the same — what changes is the historical content you're applying it to.

When a studio uses a US History standard, it must always specify both the code AND the unit:

> **US.6_12.3 + Unit 1** = Explain the cause-and-effect relationship of events during the Industrial Revolution and Progressive Era.
> **US.6_12.3 + Unit 6** = Explain the cause-and-effect relationship of events during the Civil Rights Movement.

Same skill. Different content. Always specify both.

---

## The Six Standards

| Standard | What It Measures |
|----------|-----------------|
| **US.6_12.1** | Analyze primary and secondary sources with attention to reliability, impact, and purpose |
| **US.6_12.2** | Examine the impact of multiple perspectives on social, political, and cultural development |
| **US.6_12.3** | Explain the relationship of events focusing on the link(s) between cause and effect |
| **US.6_12.4** | Compare how historical elements change over time |
| **US.6_12.5** | Analyze the significant contributions of people, policy, and the influence on an era |
| **US.6_12.6** | Connect the past to the present using current events |

### FPS Social Studies Skill Strands (Cross-Cutting)

Same three skill strands as World History — they cut across all units and standards:

| Skill Strand | What It Means |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Argumentation** | Produce well-reasoned arguments with valid, relevant, and sufficient evidence |
| **Interpretation & Analysis** | Draw accurate, relevant, and sufficient conclusions |
| **Obtain, Evaluate & Communicate (OEC)** | Research, evaluate source credibility, and communicate valid/reliable findings |
