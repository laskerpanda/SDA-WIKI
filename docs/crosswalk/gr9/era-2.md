**Grade 9 · World History Era 2 + ELA 9-10**
**Span:** 500–1600 CE · Islamic Golden Age, Crusades, Mongol Empire, Renaissance, early Reformation

## Project Ideas — Era 2

---

### 1. "Is order worth obedience?"
**Essential question:** When a political system keeps order by demanding obedience, is the trade worth it?
**WH Standard:** WH.6_12.3 + Era 2
**ELA Standard:** R.9 + W.4
**Source anchor:** Magna Carta + feudal system context
**Suggested Demo Mode:** Extended Writing or Verbal Conversation
**Possible deliverables:** Argument essay · Structured debate · Podcast comparing feudalism to another system
**The argument:** Students read the Magna Carta (what rights were demanded, by whom, from whom?) and write an argument about whether limits on government power require people with power to demand them. Direct W.4 + WH.6_12.3 crosswalk.

---

### 2. "What did the Crusades actually exchange?"
**Essential question:** If the Crusades failed as military campaigns, why do they still matter?
**WH Standard:** WH.6_12.5 + Era 2
**ELA Standard:** R.9 + W.3
**Source anchor:** CrashCourse ep-15 (Crusades)
**Suggested Demo Mode:** Extended Writing or Multimedia
**Possible deliverables:** Explanatory essay · Documentary · Annotated map with written analysis
**The argument:** John Green argues the Crusades mattered less because of who won than what was exchanged (mathematics, texts, trade goods). Students analyze his argument (R.9) and write an explanatory piece on why unintended consequences matter in history (W.3 + WH.6_12.5).

---

### 3. "Can an idea spread faster than an army?"
**Essential question:** When does an idea become powerful enough to threaten an empire?
**WH Standard:** WH.6_12.2 + Era 2
**ELA Standard:** R.9 + W.4
**Source anchor:** Luther's 95 Theses + CrashCourse ep-25 (Reformation)
**Suggested Demo Mode:** Extended Writing or Multimedia
**Possible deliverables:** Argument essay · Podcast · Video essay
**The argument:** Students analyze the 95 Theses as an argument (R.9 — what is Luther's claim? what evidence does he give?) and write their own argument about whether technology (printing press) or ideas (theology) drove the Reformation. WH.6_12.2 content + W.4 argument.

---

### 4. "When is bearing witness an act of resistance?"
**Essential question:** What does it cost someone to document an atrocity — and why does it matter?
**WH Standard:** WH.6_12.4–5 + Era 2–3
**ELA Standard:** R.9 + W.4
**Source anchor:** Las Casas — A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies
**Suggested Demo Mode:** Extended Writing or Verbal Conversation
**Possible deliverables:** Essay · Podcast · Annotated primary source analysis
**The argument:** Las Casas witnessed Spanish colonial violence and wrote about it explicitly to change policy. Students analyze his argumentative strategy (R.9 — who is he writing to? what is his evidence? what does he want the reader to do?) and write their own argument about whether witnessing and documenting harm is a moral obligation. WH.6_12.4–5 + W.4.
