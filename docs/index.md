# SDA Commons — Self-Directed Academy

**South High School · Fargo Public Schools · Grades 9–10**

This wiki is the knowledge infrastructure for the Self-Directed Academy. Students use it to understand how the program works, find skinnies, navigate standards, and access the reading library.

---

## What Is SDA?

SDA is a mastery-based pathway inside South High. Instead of earning credit by sitting in class for a semester, you earn it by **demonstrating that you actually know something**. That demonstration happens through six-week learning cycles called **Studios**.

You are enrolled in two courses simultaneously: English I or II, and either World History or US History. In SDA, these aren't separate — they're one integrated program. A single studio produces evidence for both your English standard and your Social Studies standard.

**The core equation:**

> Standard = PBC = Task → Elevate

A **standard** is the official description of a skill or concept. Every standard in SDA maps to a **Project-Based Competency (PBC)** — a concrete thing you produce to show you know it. That PBC is a **task** you can actually do. And the goal is never just to complete the task — it is to **elevate**: to get better, to go deeper, to do something worth sharing.

## The Three Spaces

SDA runs through three interconnected spaces.

### Space 1 — The Studio
A six-week learning cycle. Shared essential question, your own inquiry angle, 4–5 active standards, and a public exhibition in Week 6. See [Student Workflow](commons/student-workflow.md) for the week-by-week breakdown.

### Space 2 — The Commons
This wiki. The [Skinny Library](skinnies/index.md), [Reading Library](reading-library/index.md), studio packets, and all shared resources live here.

### Space 3 — The Command Center
The teacher-facing dashboard. It tracks your ownership level, your standard scores, and your studio progress. Your scores flow there from Headrush.

## Standards in SDA

Every studio has 4–5 active standards:

| Slot | Standard | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Social Studies | 1 WH or USH standard | Content anchor for the whole studio |
| Reading | R.2, R.4, R.7, R.8, or R.9 | Primary source + literary text analysis |
| Writing | W.3 (explanatory) or W.4 (argument) | Alternates each studio |
| Research | IR.1 + IR.2 + IR.3 + IR.4 + IR.5 (bundled) | Active every single studio |
| Communication | C.1 and/or C.6 | Assessed at the Week 6 exhibition |

## The Proficiency Scale

All standards are scored on the **NDSBL 1.0–4.0 scale**:

| Score | What It Means | Grade |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| 4.0 | Beyond the standard — inferences and applications that go further than what was taught | A (95%) |
| 3.5 | Beyond the standard with partial success | A- (91%) |
| **3.0** | **Meeting the standard — this is the target** | B (83%) |
| 2.5 | Solid 2.0 knowledge + partial 3.0 knowledge | B- (78%) |
| 2.0 | Foundation knowledge — vocabulary and basic processes, missing the complex ideas | C (73%) |
| 1.5 | Partial 2.0 knowledge | D (63%) |
| 1.0 | With help, partial understanding of 2.0 content | F (50%) |

## Student Ownership Levels

You are always at one of three levels. Your level determines how much structure you get and how much independence you have.

| Level | Name | What It Means |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **Trailblazer** | Structured support. Advisor helps narrow your inquiry angle. Proposals require approval before you begin. |
| 2 | **Maverick** | Growing independence. You propose your own angles, collaborate freely, make more of your own calls. |
| 3 | **Phoenix** | Full agency. You can propose entirely self-designed studios. Flex scheduling available. |

Levels are reviewed every two weeks. You apply to move up. See [How to SDA](commons/how-to-sda/index.md) for the full breakdown of what each level requires.
