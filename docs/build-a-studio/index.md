# Build a Studio

**SDA Commons Wiki · South High School**

This tab is for students who are ready to design their own studio — their own question, their own standard, their own plan.

Pre-built studio packets exist for good reasons. They give you structure, they put four curated texts in front of you, and they model what a strong studio looks like. Once you've run several studios and shown that you can manage the process independently, you're ready to design your own.

---

## Is This for You?

Building your own studio makes sense if:

- You've completed multiple studios and earned proficient scores
- You've shown you can manage the six-week arc — research, draft, revise, exhibit — without needing a lot of prompting
- You have a genuine question you want to investigate that doesn't fit neatly into one of the pre-built packets
- Reese has confirmed you're ready to propose your own

If you're still getting the hang of how SDA works, start with a [pre-built Studio Packet](../units/studio-archive/index.md) first. You'll get more out of designing your own once you've run the arc at least a couple of times.

**If you're not sure whether you're ready — ask Reese. That's a five-minute conversation.**

---

## What You're Actually Doing

You're making nine decisions. Once you've made all nine and your Studio Contract is signed, everything runs exactly the same as a pre-built studio — the same six-week arc, the same documents, the same exhibition. The difference is that the question and the plan are yours.

| # | Decision | You'll Do This In |
|---|----------|------------------|
| 1 | What is my Essential Question? | [Design Your Studio](design.md) |
| 2 | Which standard(s) am I targeting? | [Design Your Studio](design.md) |
| 3 | Which texts will I use? | [Design Your Studio](design.md) |
| 4 | What is my Demo mode? | [Design Your Studio](design.md) |
| 5 | What is my Deliverable? | [Your Contract](contract.md) |
| 6 | What does 3.0 look like for me, specifically? | [Your Contract](contract.md) |
| 7 | Which skinnies do I need? | [Your Contract](contract.md) |
| 8 | What are my week-by-week checkpoints? | [Your Contract](contract.md) |
| 9 | Has Reese approved my contract? | In person with Reese — end of Week 1 |

**You do not start Week 2 work until Reese has signed your contract.** That's not a formality — it protects you from six weeks of work on a question that turns out to be unanswerable or off-standard.

---

## Where to Start

1. **Start here:** [Design Your Studio](design.md) — work through your EQ, standard, texts, and demo mode before you touch the contract
2. **Then:** [Your Contract & Getting Approved](contract.md) — fill out the contract section by section, get Reese's sign-off
3. **Run your studio** — use the [Student Workflow](../commons/student-workflow.md) page as your week-by-week guide
4. **Finish:** [When You're Done](when-youre-done.md) — where finished work goes

---

## What You'll Need

By the time you sit down with your contract, you should have:

- [ ] A draft Essential Question — rough is fine, you'll refine it
- [ ] A standard in mind, or two you're deciding between
- [ ] At least two texts from the [Reading Library](../reading-library/index.md) that connect to your question
- [ ] A sense of which Demo mode fits your question and your strengths

Don't have these yet? [Design Your Studio](design.md) walks you through each one.
