# When You're Done

**SDA Commons Wiki · South High School**

You've finished your studio. Your Demo is submitted, your Reflection is complete, and Reese has given you a score. This page covers what happens next — where your work goes, how it gets recorded, and what finishing a studio actually means.

---

## Step 1 — Submit Your Demo

Your Demo of Learning is submitted to Reese directly. Depending on your demo mode:

| Demo Mode | How to submit |
|-----------|--------------|
| Written Assessment | Google Doc shared with Reese from your studio folder |
| Extended Writing | Google Doc shared with Reese from your studio folder |
| Verbal Conversation | Schedule a 10–15 minute conversation with Reese during the week you finish |
| Visual/Creative | Image or PDF uploaded to your studio folder; share with Reese |
| Multimedia/Performance | Video, podcast, or presentation file shared via Drive or presented live |
| Portfolio Annotation | Google Doc with your annotated work and commentary, shared with Reese |

**Make sure your demo is in your studio folder before you share it.** Reese scores from what's in the folder — not from an email attachment or a file you shared separately.

---

## Step 2 — Complete Your Reflection

Your Studio Reflection is a short document (2 pages) where you think back on the studio: what you learned, how the process went, and what you'd do differently. Complete it before or at the same time as you submit your Demo.

→ [Download Studio Reflection (PDF)](../assets/downloads/sda-reflection.pdf)

The Reflection has four parts:
1. **Standard & Score** — What you targeted and what you think you earned
2. **Process** — How the six weeks actually went (honestly)
3. **What You Learned** — Beyond the standard — what you understand now that you didn't before
4. **Ownership** — What you chose, what you'd choose differently, what you want to do next

Fill it out honestly. Reese reads these. He's not grading them for content — but he uses them to understand where you are and what you need next.

---

## Step 3 — File Your Work in Google Drive

All finished work goes into your studio folder in The Commons. The folder structure is:

```
The Commons
  └── [Your Subject Area]
        └── [Your Last Name, First Name]
              └── [Studio Title]
                    ├── Studio Contract — [Start Date].gdoc
                    ├── Source Log.gdoc
                    ├── [Your Demo] (essay, sketchnote, recording, etc.)
                    └── Studio Reflection.gdoc
```

If you don't have a studio folder set up, ask Reese — he'll get you into the correct shared drive.

**Everything goes in the folder before Reese signs off.** Not after, not "I'll add it later." The folder is the complete record.

## Step 4 — If This Is a Week 6 Studio — Exhibit Your Deliverable

If your contract had a Deliverable (Week 6 studio), exhibiting it is part of finishing. Your Deliverable goes to a real audience — not just to Reese.

### Common exhibition formats

**Published to the SDA Commons (digital)**
Work published to the wiki (essays, research summaries, explainers, annotated primary sources) goes through Reese. He'll add it to the appropriate section of the wiki. This is how student work becomes part of the permanent SDA Commons.

**Hallway or classroom display**
Posters, visual projects, and physical artifacts can be displayed in the SDA space or the hallway outside. Coordinate with Reese for timing — some exhibitions are set up during specific exhibition windows.

**Presentation to an audience**
If your contract listed a specific audience (other students, another class, parents, a community partner), Reese helps you coordinate the date and format. Give yourself at least a week before your target date to schedule this.

**Where does the Deliverable file go?**
Even if your Deliverable is physical or performed, put the digital version (or a photo/recording of it) in your studio folder alongside everything else.

## Step 5 — Get Your Score

Reese scores your Demo using the 1.0–4.0 scale from your contract's Proficiency Targets. He'll review:

- Your Demo
- Your Reflection
- Your Source Log

And compare all three against what you wrote in Part 5 of your contract.

**How scoring works:**

| Score | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| 1.0 | With help, you could do this. You haven't demonstrated it independently yet. |
| 2.0 | You can do the basic version independently, but haven't yet hit the full 3.0 target. |
| 3.0 | You've met the standard for this studio. This is the target. |
| 4.0 | You've gone beyond the standard in a documented, specific way. |

A 3.0 is a genuine accomplishment — it means you've proven you can do the thing the standard asks for, independently, without a template. Don't treat it as "just okay."

**If you scored lower than you hoped:**

Talk to Reese. Not to argue about the score — but to understand specifically what you didn't demonstrate and whether there's a path to revise and resubmit. In some cases, he'll allow a targeted revision. In others, he'll note it as in-progress learning and you build on it in a future studio.

## Step 6 — Recording Your Score in Headrush

*This section will be updated once Headrush is set up for SDA.*

Headrush is the platform where your demonstrations of learning are formally recorded. Once you've earned a score from Reese, it will be entered into Headrush so it counts toward your SDA record.

Until Headrush is configured, Reese tracks scores manually. Your contract, your Reflection, and the file in your studio folder are your official record.

---

## What Finishing a Studio Means

One completed studio means:

- You've earned evidence toward a standard
- You have a documented research and writing process
- You have a body of work in your Google Drive folder
- You've practiced the six-week arc — and you know what you'd do differently next time

Your reflection is your roadmap for the next studio. What you wrote in Part 4 (Ownership) is where you start planning.
