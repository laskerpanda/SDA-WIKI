# Skinny — US.6_12.6 Past to Present · Unit 9 1990s–2000s

**Standard:** US.6_12.6 — Connecting History to the Present
**Unit:** 9 — 1990s–2000s (Globalization, 9/11, Contemporary)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What from the 1990s–2000s is still present today? This is the closest unit to students' lived experience — the connections are most direct here.

---

## The Big Idea

Unit 9 isn't quite history yet — it's recent enough that many of its events are still unfolding. The Afghanistan War ended in 2021. The 2008 financial crisis shaped the economy students' families live in. The surveillance infrastructure built after 9/11 is still active. The political polarization of the 1990s accelerated through the 2010s and 2020s. Understanding Unit 9 means understanding the world students actually live in.

## The Argument Move — Capstone Standard

US.6_12.6 in Unit 9 is the capstone argument of the entire two-year course. The question isn't just what connects to the present — it's what the whole arc of American history tells us about where we are now.

**Example argument move:**
> "Students who have studied US History from industrialization to the present have seen the same patterns repeat: technological change produces economic dislocation that falls hardest on those with least power; crises produce rights violations justified as temporary that become permanent; the gap between what America claims to stand for and what it actually does is the central tension of the whole story. Understanding that pattern doesn't tell you what to do — but it tells you what questions to ask."
