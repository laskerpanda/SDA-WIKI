# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 9 1990s–2000s

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 9 — 1990s–2000s (Globalization, 9/11, Contemporary)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused 9/11, the Iraq War, and the 2008 financial crisis? What did they produce?

---

## Causes of 9/11

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| US foreign policy in Middle East | Support for Israel; troops in Saudi Arabia after Gulf War; support for authoritarian governments |
| Cold War blowback | US armed mujahideen in Afghanistan (including Bin Laden's networks) to fight Soviets |
| Al-Qaeda's ideology | Islamist extremism; opposition to US presence in Muslim lands |
| Intelligence failures | CIA and FBI had warnings they didn't connect; "failure of imagination" |

## Effects of 9/11

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Afghanistan War (2001–2021) | Longest US war in history; Taliban returns to power after US withdrawal |
| Iraq War (2003–2011) | Based on false WMD intelligence; 4,500 Americans dead; ISIS emerges from power vacuum |
| PATRIOT Act / surveillance state | Mass surveillance; Snowden revelations (2013) |
| Department of Homeland Security | New bureaucracy; TSA; security theater |
| Muslim American discrimination | Hate crimes; surveillance; civil liberties violations |
| $6+ trillion spent | Wars and security costs; national debt |

## Causes of 2008 Financial Crisis

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Financial deregulation (1990s–2000s) | Glass-Steagall repealed (1999); banks take more risk |
| Subprime mortgage lending | Banks lend to people who can't afford it; package and sell risk |
| Mortgage-backed securities | Complex financial instruments obscure the risk |
| Rating agency failure | Moody's, S&P rate toxic assets AAA |
| Housing bubble | Prices rise on speculation; inevitable collapse |

## Effects of 2008 Crisis

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| 8 million jobs lost | Worst recession since Depression |
| Bank bailouts ($700B TARP) | Unpopular; debate about who got saved |
| Dodd-Frank (2010) | Financial regulation; partially repealed 2018 |
| Tea Party / Occupy Wall Street | Populist responses on right and left |
| Income inequality accelerates | Wealth recovers faster for top than for bottom |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 9 is about blowback — actions creating their opposite.

**Example argument move:**
> "The Iraq War was intended to stabilize the Middle East and spread democracy. Its effects included: the creation of ISIS (which emerged from the power vacuum after Saddam's removal), the destabilization of the entire region, the strengthening of Iran (Saddam had been Iran's counterweight), and the erosion of American credibility globally. This is blowback — an intervention producing outcomes opposite to its stated goals. It's one of the most important causal patterns in modern history."
