# Skinny — US.6_12.3 Political Systems · Unit 8 1970s–80s

**Standard:** US.6_12.3 — Political Systems and Democratic Institutions
**Unit:** 8 — 1970s–1980s (Reagan Era, Cold War End)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did the political system change in the 1970s and 80s? What were the consequences of Watergate reform, Reagan's political realignment, and the Cold War's end?

---

## Key Political Changes — Unit 8

| Change | What Happened | Significance |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Post-Watergate reforms | Campaign finance law; ethics rules; independent counsel | Attempt to prevent future Watergate; mixed success |
| Carter presidency (1977–81) | Stagflation; Iran hostage crisis; Camp David Accords | Liberalism discredited; Reagan possible |
| Reagan revolution (1980–88) | Tax cuts; deregulation; defense buildup; "Morning in America" | Conservative coalition; South shifts to Republican |
| Iran-Contra (1986–87) | Reagan administration secretly sold arms to Iran; funded Nicaraguan rebels illegally | Potential impeachment avoided; executive power again tested |
| Deregulation | Airlines, banks, telecommunications deregulated | Savings & Loan crisis (1980s); airline competition |
| Soviet collapse (1989–91) | Berlin Wall falls Nov 9, 1989; USSR dissolves Dec 25, 1991 | Cold War ends; US sole superpower; "end of history" |
| Southern Strategy completion | South becomes solidly Republican by 1984 | Parties realign; Civil Rights backlash locked in |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.3 in Unit 8 is about realignment — the political system reorganizes.

**Example argument move:**
> "The 1980 election wasn't just a Republican victory — it was a realignment. The South, which had been solidly Democratic since the Civil War (voting against the party of Lincoln), became solidly Republican in response to Civil Rights legislation. This is the Southern Strategy completing its work. Understanding American politics today requires understanding that the parties switched positions on race — and why."
