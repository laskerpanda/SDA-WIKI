# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 8 1970s–80s

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 8 — 1970s–1980s (Reagan Era, Cold War End)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused the political and economic shifts of the 1970s and 80s? What did they produce?

---

## Causes of the 1970s Crisis

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Oil shock (OPEC embargo, 1973) | Arab states embargo US for supporting Israel in Yom Kippur War; oil prices quadruple |
| Vietnam economic cost | War spending + Great Society = inflation |
| Nixon ends gold standard (1971) | Dollar no longer convertible to gold; floating exchange rates; instability |
| Deindustrialization | Global competition; US manufacturing less competitive; jobs move |
| Watergate / post-Vietnam cynicism | Public trust in government collapses; political paralysis |

## Causes of Reagan's Rise

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Carter's failures | Stagflation; Iran hostage crisis; "malaise" speech |
| Southern realignment | Civil Rights backlash; white Southerners leave Democratic Party |
| Religious right mobilized | Roe v. Wade (1973) galvanizes evangelical political organizing |
| Tax revolt (Prop 13, 1978) | California property tax revolt; anti-government mood |

## Effects of the Reagan Era

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Tax cuts / deficit | National debt triples; supply-side economics becomes GOP orthodoxy |
| Union decline | Union membership falls from 35% to 17%; wage stagnation follows |
| Cold War end | Reagan's arms buildup + Gorbachev's reforms → Soviet collapse 1991 |
| Mass incarceration | "War on Drugs" → prison population from 500K to 2.3M by 2008 |
| Deregulation | Financial deregulation → S&L crisis and eventually 2008 crisis |
| Income inequality rise | Begins in 1980s; accelerates through present |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 8 is about long causal chains.

**Example argument move:**
> "The 2008 financial crisis was caused by financial deregulation that began in the 1980s. The deregulation was caused by Reagan-era ideology that markets self-regulate. That ideology was shaped by the stagflation of the 1970s, which discredited Keynesian economics. The 2008 crisis traces back to decisions made in 1973 and 1981. Understanding long causal chains is how you understand why the present looks the way it does."
