# Skinny — US.6_12.4 Social & Economic Change · Unit 8 1970s–80s

**Standard:** US.6_12.4 — Social and Economic Change
**Unit:** 8 — 1970s–1980s (Reagan Era, Cold War End)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did the economy and society change in the 1970s and 80s? Who gained and who lost?

---

## The Big Idea

The 1970s ended the postwar prosperity consensus. Stagflation (high inflation + high unemployment — previously thought impossible simultaneously), the oil crisis, and deindustrialization devastated the working and middle class. Reagan's response — cut taxes on wealthy, deregulate, break unions — restored growth but also dramatically increased inequality. The 1980s began the income inequality trend that has continued to the present.

---

## Key Social and Economic Changes — Unit 8

| Change | What Happened | Who Was Affected |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Stagflation (1970s) | Oil crisis + Nixon price controls → 12% inflation + 10% unemployment | Everyone; especially working/middle class |
| Deindustrialization | Manufacturing moves overseas; Rust Belt collapses | Working class; Midwest/Northeast cities |
| Union decline | Reagan fires PATCO strikers (1981); union membership falls | Working class loses wage bargaining power |
| Tax cuts (ERTA, 1981) | Top marginal rate cut from 70% to 28% | Wealthy benefit most; deficit explodes |
| Savings & Loan crisis (1980s) | Deregulated S&Ls make risky bets; 1,000+ fail; $130B bailout | Taxpayers; housing market |
| AIDS crisis (1981–) | Disproportionately affects gay men and Black/Latino communities; Reagan silent | LGBTQ+ community; delayed federal response costs lives |
| Crack cocaine epidemic | Crack hits urban communities; harsh sentencing laws | Black communities; mass incarceration begins |
| "War on Drugs" / mass incarceration | Nixon origin; Reagan escalation; mandatory minimums | Disproportionately Black and Latino; prison population explodes |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.4 in Unit 8 is about who paid for "morning in America."

**Example argument move:**
> "The Reagan economic boom of the 1980s was real — GDP grew, inflation fell, unemployment dropped. But income inequality grew simultaneously. The richest 1%'s share of income rose dramatically; working-class wages stagnated. At the same time, the crack epidemic devastated urban Black communities, the AIDS crisis went unaddressed, and mass incarceration began. 'Morning in America' depended on what time zone you were in."
