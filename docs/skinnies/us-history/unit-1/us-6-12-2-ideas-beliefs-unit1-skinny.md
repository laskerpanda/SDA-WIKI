# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 1 Industrial Revolution

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 1 — Industrial Revolution & Progressive Era (1870s–1920)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What ideas and beliefs shaped how Americans understood industrialization? What ideologies justified the new economic order — and what ideologies challenged it?

---

## The Big Idea

Two big ideas clashed during industrialization. **Social Darwinism** (Herbert Spencer, Andrew Carnegie's "Gospel of Wealth") argued that the wealthy deserved their wealth — competition was natural, inequality was the result of merit. **Progressive reform** argued the opposite: inequality was the result of an unfair system, and democracy required correcting it. The fight between these ideas shaped every political debate of the era.

---

## Key Content — Unit 1

| Idea / Belief | Core Claim | Who Held It | Effect |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Social Darwinism | Wealth = merit; poverty = weakness; don't interfere | Carnegie, Rockefeller, most politicians | Blocked labor reform for decades |
| Gospel of Wealth | Rich have a duty to use wealth for social good (charity, not redistribution) | Carnegie | Funded libraries, universities — but didn't change wages |
| Muckraker journalism | Truth-telling creates public pressure for reform | Tarbell, Sinclair, Riis | Led to antitrust laws, food safety regulations |
| Labor movement ideology | Workers have collective power; capitalism needs democratic limits | Knights of Labor, AFL | Slow gains; major backlash; red-baiting |
| Progressivism | Democracy requires active government intervention | Roosevelt, La Follette | Sherman Antitrust enforcement, FDA, income tax |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 asks you to analyze ideas as arguments. Social Darwinism wasn't just a belief — it was an argument for a policy position (don't regulate business). Progressivism was a counter-argument. When you write about this era, you're analyzing a debate.

**Example argument move:**
> "Carnegie's 'Gospel of Wealth' argued that concentrated wealth was natural and beneficial — but it conveniently justified the very conditions that made Carnegie wealthy. The Progressives argued this was ideology, not science."
