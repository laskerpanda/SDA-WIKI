# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 4 WWII

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 4 — World War II (1939–1945)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What ideologies drove WWII — fascism, democracy, imperial Japan's state ideology? How did the US use ideas to mobilize its population, and what contradictions did the war expose?

---

## The Big Idea

WWII was framed as an ideological war: democracy vs. fascism. This framing was largely accurate — but it created a contradiction. The US was fighting against racial ideology in Europe while practicing racial segregation at home. The gap between the war's stated ideals and American reality was a central tension that the Civil Rights Movement would later exploit.

## Key Ideologies — Unit 4

| Ideology | Core Claims | Who Held It |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Fascism (Nazism) | Racial hierarchy; national superiority; authoritarian state; antisemitism | Nazi Germany |
| Imperial Japan's ideology | Japanese racial/cultural superiority; Asian co-prosperity sphere; emperor as divine | Imperial Japan |
| American war ideology | Democracy vs. tyranny; freedom vs. slavery; "Four Freedoms" | US government; public |
| American contradiction | Fighting fascism while practicing racism; Japanese American internment | — |
| Anti-fascist universalism | All humans have equal dignity; Holocaust reveals cost of racial ideology | Postwar foundation for UN UDHR |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 in Unit 4 is most powerful when you hold the contradiction.

**Example argument move:**
> "FDR said America was fighting for 'freedom from fear' and 'freedom from want' for all people. Simultaneously, his administration imprisoned 120,000 Japanese Americans without charges, maintained a segregated military, and turned away Jewish refugees fleeing the Holocaust. Understanding WWII as an ideological war means grappling with the gap between what America claimed to stand for and what it actually did."
