# Skinny — US.6_12.4 Social & Economic Change · Unit 4 WWII

**Standard:** US.6_12.4 — Social and Economic Change
**Unit:** 4 — World War II (1939–1945)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did WWII transform American society? Who moved, who worked, who fought — and what changed permanently vs. what snapped back after the war?

---

## The Big Idea

WWII ended the Depression and created the modern American middle class — but not equally. Women entered the workforce in millions and were sent home when the men returned. Black Americans fought for a country that didn't treat them as equal — and returned to the same inequality. The GI Bill built white middle-class prosperity while largely excluding Black veterans. The war changed everything and left the fundamental inequality untouched.

---

## Key Social Changes — Unit 4

| Group | During the War | After the War |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Women | 6 million enter industrial workforce; "Rosie the Riveter" | Largely returned to domestic roles; Feminine Mystique builds |
| Black Americans | 1.2 million serve in segregated military; Double V campaign | Return to Jim Crow; GI Bill largely excludes them; Civil Rights builds |
| Japanese Americans | Imprisoned in internment camps; some serve in military from camps | Released 1944–45; property lost; eventual reparations (1988) |
| Working class | Full employment; wages rise; union membership peaks | GI Bill creates college-educated middle class |
| White veterans | GI Bill → college → suburbs → middle class | Postwar prosperity largely for white men |
| Black veterans | Served; returned; denied GI Bill benefits in South | Radicalized; Civil Rights Movement builds from veteran base |

**GI Bill (1944):** Provided college tuition, low-interest home loans, and job training to veterans. Transformed American society — but banks and universities discriminated, so Black veterans received far less benefit than white veterans.

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.4 in Unit 4 is about the gap between promise and reality.

**Example argument move:**
> "The GI Bill is often called the greatest social legislation in American history. For white veterans, it was — it created the suburban middle class. For Black veterans, it was largely inaccessible: banks refused mortgages in Black neighborhoods, universities refused admission, and local VA offices in the South simply didn't serve Black veterans. The same law produced completely different outcomes depending on race."
