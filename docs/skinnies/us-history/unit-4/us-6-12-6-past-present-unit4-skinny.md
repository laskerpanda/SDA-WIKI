# Skinny — US.6_12.6 Past to Present · Unit 4 WWII

**Standard:** US.6_12.6 — Connecting History to the Present
**Unit:** 4 — World War II (1939–1945)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What from WWII is still present today? How does understanding this period explain the postwar world students live in?

---

## The Big Idea

The world order students live in was designed in 1945 by the United States. The UN, NATO, IMF, World Bank, Bretton Woods currency system, nuclear deterrence, the Nuremberg Principles — all are WWII products. Every international institution that shapes current events was built in response to the catastrophe of the war.

---

## Then → Now Connections

| Then (1939–1945) | Now |
|-----------------|-----|
| United Nations (1945) | Still the primary international forum; 193 member states |
| Nuremberg Trials / international law | International Criminal Court; war crimes prosecutions (Putin, etc.) |
| NATO (1949) | Still active; Russia-Ukraine war tests it |
| Nuclear weapons (1945) | 9 nations have nuclear weapons; nonproliferation treaties |
| *Korematsu v. US* (1944) | Cited in Muslim travel ban cases (2018) |
| GI Bill model | Student loan debate; college affordability |
| Holocaust / genocide | "Never again" — used to justify interventions (Bosnia, Rwanda) |
| Holocaust denial | Remains live issue; antisemitism monitoring |
| Japanese American reparations (1988) | Model for reparations debates today |
| "The Greatest Generation" mythology | How wars are remembered; veteran treatment debates |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.6 in Unit 4 is about institutional inheritance.

**Example argument move:**
> "When Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, the international response ran entirely through institutions built in 1945: UN Security Council debate, NATO Article 5 discussions, ICC war crimes investigations, economic sanctions coordinated through Bretton Woods institutions. The world was responding to a 21st century war using tools designed after a 20th century one. Understanding WWII is understanding the architecture of the current world."
