# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 4 WWII

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 4 — World War II (1939–1945)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused WWII and US entry into it? What did the war produce — intended and unintended?

---

## Causes of WWII / US Entry

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Treaty of Versailles (1919) | Punitive terms humiliated Germany; economic devastation; Hitler exploits resentment |
| Great Depression (global) | Destroyed faith in liberal democracy; made authoritarian "solutions" attractive |
| Rise of fascism (Italy 1922, Germany 1933) | Exploited economic despair and nationalist grievance |
| Appeasement policy | Britain/France allow Hitler's territorial expansion hoping to avoid war; Munich 1938 |
| Invasion of Poland (Sept 1, 1939) | Britain and France declare war on Germany |
| Pearl Harbor (Dec 7, 1941) | Japan attacks US Pacific fleet; US declares war on Japan; Germany declares war on US |
| Atlantic Charter (1941) | US and Britain align before formal US entry |

## Effects of WWII

| Effect | Domain | Significance |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| 60+ million dead (global) | Human | Largest death toll in human history |
| Holocaust — 6 million Jews murdered | Human | Genocide as deliberate state policy |
| Atomic bombs; Japan surrenders | Military/Political | New era of nuclear weapons |
| US becomes global superpower | Geopolitical | Britain, France, USSR weakened; US economy intact |
| Cold War begins | Geopolitical | WWII alliance splits into two-superpower world |
| United Nations founded (1945) | International | New framework for international law and cooperation |
| Nuremberg Principles | Legal | International law of war crimes; "following orders" rejected |
| Decolonization wave | Global | WWII weakens European empires; independence movements accelerate |
| American economic dominance | Economic | US produces 50% of world GDP in 1945 |
| GI Bill / middle class | Domestic | Postwar prosperity; suburbs; college enrollment explodes |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 4 invites a multi-level causal argument.

**Example argument move:**
> "The most consequential unintended effect of WWII was the Cold War. The US and USSR had been suspicious allies. The war gave them both reason to maintain large military establishments and created a power vacuum in Europe that both rushed to fill. Nobody planned the Cold War — it was the war's most important unintended consequence."
