# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 7 Vietnam Era

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 7 — Vietnam Era & Watergate (1964–1974)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What ideologies drove US involvement in Vietnam — and what ideologies drove the opposition? What did Watergate reveal about the beliefs underlying executive power?

---

## The Big Idea

Vietnam was fought in the name of containment ideology — stopping communism from spreading. The anti-war movement challenged that ideology, arguing the war was unwinnable, unjust, and based on deception. The clash produced the most significant domestic political fracture since the Civil War. Watergate then revealed that the administration believed it could operate above the law — and was wrong.

---

## Key Ideologies — Unit 7

| Ideology | Core Claim | Who Held It |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Containment / domino theory | Communism must be stopped everywhere; Vietnam falling means Southeast Asia falls | LBJ, Nixon, military establishment |
| Anti-war ideology | War is unwinnable; based on deception; immoral; waste of lives | Students, veterans (VVAW), civil rights leaders |
| MLK's anti-war stance (1967) | War is connected to racism and poverty; can't fight for freedom abroad while denying it at home | MLK; moral/religious anti-war argument |
| Nixon's "silent majority" | Most Americans support the war; protesters are fringe | Nixon strategy; backlash politics |
| Imperial presidency | Executive has vast secret powers in national security; Congress and courts shouldn't interfere | Nixon White House |
| Constitutional constraint | No one is above the law; executive power has limits | Congress, courts during Watergate |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 in Unit 7 is about ideology colliding with reality.

**Example argument move:**
> "The domino theory required believing that if Vietnam fell to communism, all of Southeast Asia would follow. After Vietnam fell in 1975, Cambodia went communist. Laos went communist. Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia did not. The domino theory was partly wrong — which means the argument used to justify 58,000 American deaths was built on a prediction that didn't fully come true."
