# Skinny — US.6_12.3 Political Systems · Unit 7 Vietnam Era

**Standard:** US.6_12.3 — Political Systems and Democratic Institutions
**Unit:** 7 — Vietnam Era & Watergate (1964–1974)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did the Vietnam War and Watergate test American democratic institutions? What constitutional questions did they raise — and how were they resolved?

---

## The Big Idea

Watergate is the clearest test of American democratic institutions in the 20th century — and the institutions held. Nixon believed the President could authorize illegal acts in the name of national security. The Congress, courts, and press proved him wrong. But the era also showed how close the system came to failing.

---

## Key Constitutional Moments — Unit 7

| Event | Constitutional Issue | Resolution |
|-------|---------------------|------------|
| Gulf of Tonkin Resolution (1964) | Congress authorized war based on exaggerated/false report | War Powers Resolution (1973) attempted to prevent recurrence |
| Pentagon Papers (1971) | Government tried to suppress publication; press freedom vs. national security | Supreme Court rules 6-3 for press; *NY Times v. US* |
| Watergate break-in (1972) | Campaign operatives break into opposition HQ; White House covers up | Nixon resigns; aides convicted |
| Saturday Night Massacre (Oct 1973) | Nixon fires special prosecutor; two AGs resign rather than obey | House begins impeachment inquiry |
| Nixon tapes subpoena | Nixon claims executive privilege; refuses to release tapes | Supreme Court rules 8-0 tapes must be released; *US v. Nixon* |
| Nixon resignation (Aug 9, 1974) | Faced certain impeachment and conviction; resigns | First presidential resignation in US history |
| Ford pardons Nixon (Sept 1974) | Ford prevents Nixon prosecution | Controversial; Ford loses 1976 election partly because of it |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.3 in Unit 7 is about institutional resilience — the system works, barely.

**Example argument move:**
> "Watergate is often taught as a civics lesson about how American democracy works. But a more accurate lesson is how close it came to not working. Nixon had 18 months of cover-up during which he could have destroyed the tapes. The House moved slowly on impeachment. The Saturday Night Massacre showed a president willing to fire anyone who stood in his way. The institutions held — but they held because individuals within them made courageous choices, not because the rules automatically prevailed."
