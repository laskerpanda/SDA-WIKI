# Skinny — US.6_12.1 Science & Technology · Unit 7 Vietnam Era

**Standard:** US.6_12.1 — Science, Technology, and Society
**Unit:** 7 — Vietnam Era & Watergate (1964–1974)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did technology shape the Vietnam War — and how did media technology change how Americans experienced it?

---

## The Big Idea

Vietnam was the first televised war. For the first time in American history, combat footage appeared on nightly news broadcasts. The gap between the government's claims ("we are winning") and what Americans saw on their televisions ("we are not winning") created the "credibility gap" that destroyed public support for the war. Technology didn't cause the war — but television ended it politically.

---

## Key Technologies — Unit 7

| Technology | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Television (evening news) | Brought war footage into living rooms; Tet Offensive coverage pivotal |
| Napalm | Incendiary weapon; burns on contact with water; civilians affected; iconic photographs |
| Agent Orange | Herbicide defoliant; destroyed jungle cover; long-term health effects on veterans |
| Helicopters | Changed battlefield mobility; air cavalry; medevac |
| Body count metrics | Misused technology of measurement; created perverse incentives |
| Photocopier (Pentagon Papers) | Ellsberg photocopied classified documents; enabled leak |
| Recording equipment (Nixon tapes) | Nixon's own taping system provided the evidence that ended his presidency |

**The Pentagon Papers (1971):** Daniel Ellsberg photocopied 7,000 pages of classified Defense Department history of Vietnam, showing systematic government deception. He gave them to the *New York Times*. Nixon tried to suppress publication; Supreme Court ruled 6-3 for publication. First major First Amendment / classified information case.

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.1 in Unit 7 is about technology as truth-teller — or truth-revealer.

**Example argument move:**
> "Nixon's downfall came from his own technology. He installed a taping system to create a historical record — and the tapes proved he had ordered the Watergate cover-up. The Pentagon Papers were leaked because a photocopier existed. The Vietnam War's support collapsed because of television. In the 1960s and 70s, technology kept creating evidence that governments would rather not have existed."
