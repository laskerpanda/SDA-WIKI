# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 2 Imperialism & WWI

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 2 — Imperialism & World War I (1890s–1919)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused American imperialism? What caused US entry into WWI? What did each produce?

---

## The Big Idea

Imperialism and WWI both followed from the same underlying cause: American industrial power needed markets and resources beyond its borders. The effects cascaded across politics, society, and international relations — and set up every conflict of the 20th century.

---

## Causes of American Imperialism

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Economic expansion | Industrial overproduction needed overseas markets |
| Alfred Thayer Mahan's sea power theory | Naval power = global power; need coaling stations; need colonies |
| Social Darwinism / racial ideology | "Civilized" nations have right/duty to govern "lesser" ones |
| Strategic competition | Britain, France, Germany building empires; US falls behind or joins |
| Yellow journalism | Hearst and Pulitzer newspapers inflame public opinion about Cuba |

## Causes of US Entry into WWI

| Cause | Weight |
|-------|--------|
| Economic ties to Britain/France | Heavy; US banks had loaned Allied powers $2.3 billion |
| Submarine warfare / Lusitania | Gradual; Germany sinks US ships |
| Zimmermann Telegram | Immediate trigger; Germany proposes Mexico attack US |
| Wilson's idealism | "Make the world safe for democracy" |

## Effects — Imperialism

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Spanish-American War (1898) | US gains Cuba, Philippines, Puerto Rico, Guam |
| Anti-imperialist movement | Mark Twain, Carnegie argue against empire |
| Philippine-American War (1899–1902) | 200,000+ Filipino deaths; brutal suppression of independence movement |
| Panama Canal (1914) | Strategic and economic asset; acquired through quasi-imperial means |

## Effects — WWI

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Treaty of Versailles | Punishes Germany; plants seeds of WWII |
| League of Nations | Wilson's project; Senate rejects US membership |
| Great Migration | Black workers move north |
| Women's suffrage (1920) | War contribution argument clinches it |
| Red Scare (1919–20) | Anti-communist hysteria; Palmer Raids |
| Immigration restriction (1921, 1924) | Nativist backlash; quotas severely limit Eastern European immigration |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 2 is richest when you trace unintended consequences. *The war to end all wars planted the seeds of the next one.*

**Example argument move:**
> "The Treaty of Versailles is the clearest example in American history of a military victory producing the conditions for a worse conflict. The US helped win WWI, then refused to join the League of Nations that might have managed the peace. Twenty years later, the consequences arrived."
