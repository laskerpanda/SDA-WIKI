# Skinny — US.6_12.1 Science & Technology · Unit 2 Imperialism & WWI

**Standard:** US.6_12.1 — Science, Technology, and Society
**Unit:** 2 — Imperialism & World War I (1890s–1919)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did new technologies shape American imperialism and the conduct of WWI? What did these technologies make possible — and what did they make terrible?

---

## The Big Idea

Technology enabled American imperial expansion (steam-powered navies, telegraph cables, weapons) and made WWI uniquely destructive (machine guns, poison gas, artillery barrages, trench warfare). The same industrial technology that built American prosperity was applied to warfare — with results that shocked everyone who experienced it.

---

## Key Content — Unit 2

| Technology | Role in Imperialism | Role in WWI |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Steam-powered warships | Projected US military power into Pacific and Caribbean | Naval warfare; submarine warfare |
| Telegraph / undersea cables | Enabled coordination of empire; US controlled key cables | Military command and communication |
| Machine gun | — | Made frontal assault suicidal; created stalemate |
| Poison gas (chlorine, mustard) | — | New horror; violated previous rules of war |
| Artillery | — | 60% of WWI casualties; destroyed landscape |
| Airplanes | — | Reconnaissance; early bombing; air combat |
| Submarines | — | German U-boats sink merchant ships; key US entry trigger |

---

## The Argument Move

Technology in Unit 2 is most powerful as a cause-and-effect argument: *the same industrial capacity that made America a great power also made the war it entered into the most destructive in human history.*

**Example argument move:**
> "The industrialization of warfare produced a paradox: the countries with the most advanced technology fought to a bloody stalemate for four years. Machine guns made attack suicidal; artillery reduced villages to rubble; poison gas violated every previous understanding of acceptable combat. Technology didn't produce quick victory — it produced the Western Front."
