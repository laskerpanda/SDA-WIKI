# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 2 Imperialism & WWI

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 2 — Imperialism & World War I (1890s–1919)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What ideas justified American imperialism and entry into WWI? How did war propaganda work — and what beliefs did it create or reinforce?

---

## The Big Idea

American imperialism was justified by a cluster of ideas: racial hierarchy ("the White Man's Burden"), Manifest Destiny extended beyond US borders, Social Darwinism applied internationally (strong nations should dominate weak ones), and religious mission ("civilizing" non-Christian peoples). WWI was sold to the American public through propaganda — posters, speeches, films — that created a simple moral story out of a complicated geopolitical conflict.

---

## Key Ideas — Unit 2

| Idea | Core Claim | Used To Justify |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| "White Man's Burden" | Racial hierarchy; "civilized" nations have duty to govern "lesser" peoples | Philippine annexation, Caribbean interventions |
| Monroe Doctrine / Roosevelt Corollary | US has right to intervene in Latin America | Cuba, Panama, Haiti, Dominican Republic |
| Manifest Destiny (extended) | America's expansion is divinely ordained | Pacific territories; Philippines |
| Wilsonian idealism | WWI is a war to "make the world safe for democracy" | US entry into WWI; Fourteen Points |
| War propaganda | Simple moral story: we are good, they are evil | Draft, war bonds, public support for war |
| Anti-war / Sedition | Some Americans believed war was corporate-driven | Espionage Act prosecutions |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 in Unit 2 is about interrogating official justifications. The most powerful argument: *the ideas used to justify imperialism and war were not neutral beliefs — they were arguments that served specific interests.*

**Example argument move:**
> "Kipling's 'White Man's Burden' wasn't just a poem — it was an argument for a policy position. It claimed that racial hierarchy was natural and imperial domination was a moral obligation. H.T. Johnson's rebuttal showed that the same facts could produce the opposite conclusion. Ideology is always an argument."
