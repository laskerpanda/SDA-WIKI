# Skinny — US.6_12.4 Social & Economic Change · Unit 2 Imperialism & WWI

**Standard:** US.6_12.4 — Social and Economic Change
**Unit:** 2 — Imperialism & World War I (1890s–1919)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

How did imperialism and WWI reshape American society? Who moved, who worked, who fought — and what happened to them?

---

## The Big Idea

WWI transformed American society in ways that outlasted the war. Women moved into the industrial workforce in large numbers. Black Americans began the Great Migration northward, escaping Jim Crow and finding war-industry jobs. Immigrants from Southern and Eastern Europe faced new nativist pressure. The war demanded sacrifice from everyone — but not equally.

---

## Key Social Changes — Unit 2

| Group | Experience |
|-------|-----------|
| Women | War industries employ women in factories; suffrage movement gains momentum; 19th Amendment (1920) follows |
| Black Americans | Great Migration begins (500,000 north 1916–1920); factory jobs; still face discrimination in North |
| Immigrants | War-era nativism; German Americans face hostility; immigration restriction builds momentum |
| Working class | War production raises wages temporarily; labor rights expand slightly |
| Soldiers | 117,000 Americans die; many return with physical/psychological wounds; "shell shock" |
| Colonized peoples | Philippines, Puerto Rico, Guam — absorbed into US empire; limited self-determination |

**Economic effects of WWI:**
- US shifts from debtor to creditor nation
- American businesses profit enormously from Allied war contracts (pre-1917)
- War production ends the labor surplus; wages rise temporarily
- War industries draw workers from South and rural areas → urbanization accelerates

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.4 in Unit 2 asks whose social position changed and why. The richest argument: *the war created opportunities for some groups (women, Black workers) while simultaneously exposing the limits of those opportunities (they weren't structural changes — they were temporary).*

**Example argument move:**
> "The Great Migration was one of the most significant demographic shifts in American history — and it happened because of WWI. Black Americans didn't move north because conditions improved; they moved because war industries needed workers and the South's Jim Crow system had become intolerable. The North was better, but not equal. Understanding the Great Migration means understanding both the pull and the limits of that pull."
