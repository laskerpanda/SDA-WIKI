# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 5 Cold War

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 5 — Cold War (1945–1991)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What ideologies drove the Cold War? How did anti-communism shape American culture, politics, and civil liberties — and what did American literature say about it?

---

## The Big Idea

The Cold War was an ideological war: capitalism + democracy vs. communism + authoritarianism. But the American side's commitment to democracy was tested by McCarthyism — a period of political persecution that used fear of communism to destroy careers and silence dissent. The literature of the Cold War (*1984*, *Fahrenheit 451*) responded to exactly this — the danger of conformism and the suppression of thought.

---

## Key Ideologies — Unit 5

| Ideology | Core Claims | Effect on America |
|----------|------------|------------------|
| Anti-communism / containment | Communism must be stopped everywhere it tries to spread | Korea, Vietnam, proxy wars worldwide |
| McCarthyism | Communist sympathizers are everywhere; loyalty must be proven | 1950–54; Hollywood blacklist; careers destroyed; civil liberties curtailed |
| Domino theory | If one country goes communist, neighbors follow | Justification for Vietnam |
| Massive retaliation | Threaten nuclear response to Soviet aggression | Nuclear arms race escalation |
| Mutually Assured Destruction | Both sides destroyed in nuclear war → nobody launches | Deterrence strategy; stable but terrifying |
| Liberalism vs. conservatism | Cold War era reshapes both parties | GOP becomes anti-communist party; Democrats split |

**The literary response:**
- *1984* (1949) — Orwell writes about Soviet-style totalitarianism but the critique applies to any conformist state
- *Fahrenheit 451* (1953) — Bradbury responds directly to McCarthyism and book-burning
- Both books became central Cold War texts — and are still assigned today

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 in Unit 5 is about ideology creating its own dangers.

**Example argument move:**
> "McCarthyism shows what happens when the ideology of fighting tyranny becomes tyrannical itself. McCarthy destroyed careers based on accusation alone, in the name of protecting freedom. *Fahrenheit 451* and *1984* were written in response to exactly this pattern — the book-burners always believe they're burning books to protect something. That's the ideology of McCarthyism."
