# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 5 Cold War

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 5 — Cold War (1945–1991)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused the Cold War? What were its major effects — on American society, foreign policy, and the international system?

---

## Causes of the Cold War

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| WWII alliance fracture | US and USSR were allies of necessity; fundamentally different systems |
| Soviet sphere in Eastern Europe | USSR establishes communist governments in occupied nations |
| Nuclear weapons / US monopoly | US had bomb (1945); USSR gets it (1949); arms race begins |
| Ideological incompatibility | Democracy + capitalism vs. communism + authoritarianism |
| Truman Doctrine (1947) | US commits to opposing communist expansion globally |
| Berlin Blockade (1948–49) | USSR blockades West Berlin; US airlifts supplies; showdown |

## Key Cold War Events and Effects

| Event | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Korean War (1950–53) | 36,000 Americans die; 38th parallel restored; "limited war" concept |
| McCarthy era (1950–54) | Civil liberties crisis; careers destroyed; conformism enforced |
| Sputnik (1957) | Science education reform; NASA; space race |
| Bay of Pigs (1961) | Failed CIA invasion of Cuba; US credibility damaged |
| Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) | Closest to nuclear war; back-channel deal; "eyeball to eyeball" |
| Vietnam War (1965–73) | 58,000 Americans die; massive social upheaval; military credibility damaged |
| Nixon-China (1972) | US recognizes China; triangular diplomacy; détente |
| Soviet collapse (1991) | Cold War ends; US becomes sole superpower; "end of history" claimed |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 5 invites argument about who "won" — and what that means.

**Example argument move:**
> "The US 'won' the Cold War, but the winning was complicated. Vietnam cost 58,000 American lives and deeply damaged public trust in government. McCarthyism damaged civil liberties for a decade. The military-industrial complex Eisenhower warned about became permanent. And the Soviet collapse didn't produce the stable democratic world promised — it produced a power vacuum that enabled authoritarian resurgence. Winning had costs."
