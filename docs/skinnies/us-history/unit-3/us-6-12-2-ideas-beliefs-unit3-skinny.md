# Skinny — US.6_12.2 Ideas, Beliefs & Ideology · Unit 3 Roaring 20s & Depression

**Standard:** US.6_12.2 — Ideas, Beliefs, and Institutions
**Unit:** 3 — Roaring 20s & Great Depression (1919–1939)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What competing ideas defined the 1920s — modernism vs. traditionalism, prosperity vs. reform? How did the Depression change what Americans believed about the economy and government?

---

## The Big Idea

The 1920s were a decade of cultural war: modernism (evolution, jazz, women's liberation, immigration) vs. traditionalism (Prohibition, the KKK's revival, the Scopes Trial, immigration restriction). The Depression killed the idea that the economy was self-correcting and individual virtue was enough. Keynesian economics — government as economic manager — replaced laissez-faire as the dominant framework.

---

## Key Ideas — Unit 3

| Idea / Movement | Core Claim | Significance |
|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| Modernism / Jazz Age | Tradition should be challenged; new is better; freedom from Victorian norms | Harlem Renaissance; flapper culture; secularism |
| Nativism / KKK revival | America is for "real Americans" (white, Protestant, native-born) | Immigration acts of 1921/1924; racial terror |
| Fundamentalism (Scopes Trial) | Bible is literal truth; evolution threatens society | Scopes convicted; modernism wins culturally |
| Prohibition | Alcohol is moral evil; government can legislate morality | Organized crime grows; eventually repealed (1933) |
| Harlem Renaissance | Black art, literature, music as affirmation of dignity and identity | Hughes, Hurston, Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington |
| Keynesianism | Government spending can stabilize the economy | FDR's New Deal; permanent shift in economic thinking |
| Laissez-faire (Hoover) | Economy self-corrects; government should not interfere | Discredited by Depression; Hoover loses 1932 |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.2 in Unit 3 is about a battle of ideas — and one side losing dramatically.

**Example argument move:**
> "The Depression didn't just destroy wealth — it destroyed a belief system. Hoover's faith in laissez-faire economics — that the market would self-correct and charity would handle the rest — was tested by reality and failed. FDR won in 1932 not just because of the Depression, but because Hoover had no ideological response to it. The Depression made Keynesianism possible."
