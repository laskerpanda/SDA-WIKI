# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 3 Roaring 20s & Depression

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 3 — Roaring 20s & Great Depression (1919–1939)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused the Great Depression? What did it produce — and what ended it?

---

## The Big Idea

The Great Depression had multiple, interconnected causes — no single factor explains it. Its effects reshaped American government, society, and culture in ways that lasted for decades. Understanding the Depression requires holding multiple causes simultaneously and tracing effects across different domains.

---

## Causes of the Great Depression

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Stock market speculation / Black Tuesday | Buying on margin; overleveraged investors; crash triggers panic |
| Bank failures | 9,000 banks fail 1930–33; deposits uninsured; savings wiped out |
| Overproduction / underconsumption | Factories producing more than Americans could afford to buy |
| Agricultural crisis | Farmers had been struggling since 1920; crop prices fell post-WWI |
| Smoot-Hawley Tariff (1930) | Raised import tariffs; trading partners retaliate; international trade collapses |
| Federal Reserve contraction | Fed raised interest rates during the crisis; made it worse |
| Consumer credit collapse | Buying on credit stopped when confidence fell; demand collapsed |

## Effects of the Great Depression

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| 25% unemployment (peak 1933) | 1 in 4 workers without jobs |
| Bank failures / savings lost | Middle-class savings wiped out |
| Dust Bowl / migration | 500,000 displaced; Okies to California |
| Hoovervilles | Shantytowns of unemployed; visible public poverty |
| New Deal | Permanent expansion of federal government |
| WWII ends it | War production employs everyone; not New Deal programs |
| Social Security, FDIC | Permanent safety net built from Depression experience |
| Political shift | FDR coalition (unions, Black voters, immigrants, South) shapes Democratic Party for 40 years |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 3 invites a causal argument about complexity.

**Example argument move:**
> "The single most important cause of the Depression was the Federal Reserve's decision to raise interest rates after the crash. By contracting the money supply when the economy needed liquidity, the Fed turned a serious recession into a decade-long catastrophe. Economist Milton Friedman argued this 40 years later. The Fed has never made the same mistake again — which is why recent recessions haven't become depressions."
