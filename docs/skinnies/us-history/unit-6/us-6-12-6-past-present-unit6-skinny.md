# Skinny — US.6_12.6 Past to Present · Unit 6 Civil Rights

**Standard:** US.6_12.6 — Connecting History to the Present
**Unit:** 6 — Civil Rights Movement (1954–1968)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What from the Civil Rights Movement is still present today? How does understanding this period explain current debates about race, equality, and justice?

---

## Then → Now Connections

| Then (1954–1968) | Now |
|-----------------|-----|
| Voting Rights Act (1965) | *Shelby County v. Holder* (2013) guts key provisions; voter ID laws |
| Fair Housing Act (1968) | Racial wealth gap in homeownership persists |
| Redlining | Federal Reserve studies show redlined neighborhoods still lag today |
| Black Power / self-determination | Black Lives Matter movement; community organizing |
| MLK's "Letter from Birmingham Jail" | Still cited in debates about protest tactics |
| *Brown v. Board* (1954) | School resegregation debate; charter schools; school choice |
| Civil disobedience as tactic | Protests worldwide; Standing Rock; climate activism |
| White backlash → Southern Strategy | Political realignment of South; culture war politics |
| Police violence against Black Americans | Same pattern as Civil Rights era; now on video |
| "I Have a Dream" (selective memory) | MLK quoted to oppose things he supported |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.6 in Unit 6 is about what "incomplete" means.

**Example argument move:**
> "The Voting Rights Act of 1965 was called the movement's crowning achievement. In 2013, the Supreme Court ruled in *Shelby County v. Holder* that the formula used to determine which states needed federal oversight was outdated — effectively gutting the law. Within hours of the ruling, several states passed new voting restrictions. Understanding the Civil Rights Movement means understanding that its victories were not permanent — they require ongoing defense."
