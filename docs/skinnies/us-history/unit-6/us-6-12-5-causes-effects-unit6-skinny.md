# Skinny — US.6_12.5 Causes & Effects · Unit 6 Civil Rights

**Standard:** US.6_12.5 — Causes and Effects of Historical Events
**Unit:** 6 — Civil Rights Movement (1954–1968)
**Grade:** 10 · US History

---

## What This Standard Asks

What caused the Civil Rights Movement? What did it produce — immediate and long-term?

---

## Causes of the Civil Rights Movement

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Accumulated injustice | Jim Crow laws; lynching; disenfranchisement; economic exclusion — decades of organized oppression |
| WWII veterans radicalized | Black men fought for freedom abroad, returned to Jim Crow; demanded consistency |
| Great Migration (North) | Black Americans in Northern cities had more political power; Northern white liberals as potential allies |
| *Brown v. Board* (1954) | Legal victory showed courts could be used; built momentum |
| Emmett Till murder (1955) | Publicized horror of racial violence; galvanized movement |
| Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955) | Showed economic pressure works; MLK emerges |
| Cold War context | US fighting for "freedom" internationally while practicing apartheid domestically; international embarrassment |

## Effects of the Civil Rights Movement

| Effect | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Civil Rights Act (1964) | Most comprehensive anti-discrimination law ever |
| Voting Rights Act (1965) | Black voter registration dramatically increases |
| Fair Housing Act (1968) | Housing discrimination prohibited |
| Cultural shift | Black culture enters mainstream American culture |
| White backlash / Southern Strategy | Nixon's 1968 campaign exploits white resentment of Civil Rights |
| Black Power movement | Radicalization of younger activists; Malcolm X; Black Panthers |
| Women's movement | Civil Rights Movement provides model and language |

---

## The Argument Move

US.6_12.5 in Unit 6 is about strategic causation — the movement understood what would produce change.

**Example argument move:**
> "The Civil Rights Movement's most important strategic insight was this: moral suasion alone doesn't produce change, but moral suasion combined with economic pressure and publicized violence does. The Montgomery Bus Boycott cost the city money. The Birmingham campaign made segregationists look barbaric on television. The March on Washington put 250,000 people on the National Mall. Each was designed to produce a specific political effect. The movement understood causation."
